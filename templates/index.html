Counterfeiting Detection Of Banking Using Machine Learning



The proliferation of digital transactions in banking has led to an increase in fraudulent activities, posing significant challenges for financial institutions. To combat this, machine learning techniques have emerged as effective tools for fraud detection. The proposed approach involves the utilization of historical transaction data to train machine learning models capable of identifying fraudulent patterns. Various algorithms such as logistic regression, decision trees, random forests and support vector machines, are explored for their effectiveness in detecting fraudulent transactions. Feature engineering techniques are employed to extract relevant information from transaction data, enhancing the models' predictive capabilities.
